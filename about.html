About

I am currently located in Canada working on antimicrobial resistance (AMR) surveillance in the health care system. The work lies at the intersection of infectious disease ecology, immunology, and public health.

I completed my PhD in Immunology and Infectious Diseases at Cornell University and hold an MPH in Epidemiology and Biostatistics from Columbia University. Before my current position, I spent several years working on viral pathogen spillover (cross species pathogen transmission). When possible, I try to work across disciplines (field work, experimental infection models, and computational approaches), it keeps things interesting.
